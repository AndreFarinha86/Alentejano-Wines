Our Brands

Red

Borba Grande Reserve

Grape varieties: Trincadeira and Alicante Bouschet.

Ageing: The ageing was performed for 18 months in new tanks for the wine-wood integration. After a slight filtration the wine was bottled and stored for 12 months in the cellar.

Alcohol: 14,5%Vol.

How to serve: At 15-17 ºC.

Gastronomy: Ideal to accompany elaborate dishes (black pork, Alentejo veal and game)and desserts.

Consumption: This wine is to be consumed preferably after bottling, but it can also age for another 15 years.

Herdade das Barras

Grape varieties: Cabernet Sauvignon, Alicante Bouschet and Syra.

Ageing: American and french oak barrels: 24 months. Bottle: 6 months (minimum).

Alcohol: 14.5%Vol.

How to serve: At 15-16 ºC.

Gastronomy: It combines well with meat dishes and baked or grilled cheese.

Consumption: Can be stored for more than 10 years in a cool dry place, in a horizontal position under the sunligh.

7,80

Monte da Cal

Grape varieties: 40% Touriga Nacional, 40% Aragonez and 20% Alicante Bouschet.

Ageing: Crush with total destemming, fermentation at 26°C, long skin maceration, 12 months in french oak barrels and 6 months in bottle.

Alcohol: 14.5%Vol.

How to serve: At 15-17 ºC.

Gastronomy: Serve with stewed or roasted red meats, all types of game and well-structured cheese.

Consumption: 7 to 10 years.

White

Comendador Reserve

Grape varieties: Antão Vaz, Roupeiro and Arinto.

Ageing: Busches harvested manually and submitted to selection at the vineyard and winery. Fermentation in new rench and American oak barrels followed by aging for 6 month with batonnage.

Alcohol: 13%Vol.

How to serve: At 8-10 ºC.

Gastronomy: Shellfish elaborately prepared. Dogfish soup. Rich fish.

Consumption: 3 to 6 years.

Arrepidao Velho

Grape varieties: 70% Antão Vaz, 10% Viognier, 10% Verdelho and 10% Riesling

Ageing: Grapes were selectively handpicked into 20 kg harvest boxes and transported to a refrigerated chamber in the winery. The wine was made by gentle whole bunch pressing and a totally natural clarification giving the wine a real purity of fruit and absence of harsh phenolic compounds. The juice was slowly fermented, at 12 ºC.

Alcohol: 13%Vol.

How to serve: At 10-12 ºC.

Gastronomy: Well matched with dishes that require a wine that has both body and freshnesssuch as grilled fish and simple dishes of pasta al dente.

Consumption:

Monte da Cal Saturnino Grande Reserve

Grape varieties: 50% Alvarinho, 25% Antão Vaz and 25% Arinto

Ageing: Total destemming, soft pneumatic pressing, natural static clarification and fermentation at 15°C. Fifty percent of wine was fermented in new oak barrels with soft toast. 6 months in French Oak barrels with battonage and 6 months in bottle.

Alcohol: 13.5%Vol.

How to serve: At 9-12 ºC.

Gastronomy: Miscellaneous meats, white meat grilled or in the oven, grilled red meat, cooked fish, and soft cheeses.

Consumption: 2 to 3 years

Rose

Monte da Raposinha

Grape varieties: 50% Touriga Nacional, 50% Aragonez

Ageing: Hand-harvested and sorted at the winery, this clusters were destemmed, slightly crushed and went directly to the press for a very soft pressing. Most of the must fermented in stainless steel tanks was free-run must, to preserve all the freshness and pure fruit from the grapes.

Alcohol: 12.5%Vol.

How to serve: At 8-10 ºC

Gastronomy: This dry style rosé is very refreshing, gastronomic and delicate. Perfect to go along with fish, seafood and pasta dishes.

Consumption: Best consumed after bottling or stored up to 2 – 3 years.

Borba

Grape varieties: Aragonez and Syrah.

Ageing: The grapes were destemmed and slightly crushed, process after which a peculiar maceration was performed in stainless steel vats for 12 hours at a controlled temperature of 10ºC. After the sangria and slight pressing, the tear must ferments in stainless steel vats at a controlled temperature of 15º. The stabilisation through the cold method and the final filtration then follow. The wine is immediately bottled to maintain its fruitiness, freshness and persistence in flavour.

Alcohol: 12.5%Vol.

How to serve: At 8-10 ºC

Gastronomy: Accompaniment with seafood, grilled fresh fish, white meat or aperitif.

Consumption: Best consumed after bottling or stored up to 2 – 3 years.

6,30

Take a view in our experiences

Alentejo has a predominantly Mediterranean climate - hot, dry and sunny, with large continental influence, which translates into unique conditions for the vineyard cultivation. The landscape is made up of wide plains and hills, with a predominance of clay, granite, limestone, and schist soil.

Alentejo demarcated region is divided into eight sub-regions: Reguengos, Borba, Redondo, Vidigueira, Évora, Granja-Amareleja, Portalegre and Moura. It is also grouped in three poles: Portalegre, center and south Alentejo.

In the Portalegre region the microclimate is influenced by the low temperatures of São Mamede Mountain, where the main vineyards of this region are planted on the steep mountain hillsides and on granite soils.

In the centre of Alentejo are the sub-regions of Borba, Reguengos, Redondo and Évora. Borba is the second largest sub-region of Alentejo. Reguengos is one of the main sub-regions of Alentejo, with Reguengos de Monsaraz as its ex-libris, elected the European Wine City in 2015.

In the south of Alentejo, hotter and drier, are the sub-regions of Moura, Vidigueira and Granja-Amareleja.

In order to get to know the region's food and wine culture nothing better than visiting a winery, where you can learn about the whole process of winemaking, visit the facilities, the vineyards as well as taste the true nectar, accompanied by the local gastronomy delicacies. The region also provides a wide range of leisure activities related to wine, such as, the Festival of Vine and Wine, in Borba, which takes places every year in summer. Other places to visit are Redondo Wine Museum, where several aspects of Alentejo winemaking are displayed, or also the Alentejo Wine Route Tasting Room, in Évora, a World Heritage City.

The Alentejo Wine Route can be subdivided into three main routes: " São Mamede Route", "Historical Route" and "Guadiana Route". These routes enable you to experience all the richness of this wide and wonderful region. The "São Mamede Route", located in the area covered by the Natural Park of São Mamede Mountain, includes some of the most beautiful and typical Alentejo cities and villages, such as Portalegre, Marvão, Castelo de Vide, Alter do Chão or Sousel. The "Historical Route" is centred in Évora but also comprises the areas of Arraiolos, Estremoz, Borba, Vila Viçosa, Redondo and Monsaraz. At last, the "Guadiana Route" marked by the presence of the river with this very name and by the vast plains, ranging Mourão, Moura, Vidigueira, Alvito and Viana do Alentejo.

The Alentejo regional gastronomy is synonymous with flavour, intensity and passion, the açordas or bread soups, migas (cornbread dish), gazpacho, lamb stew, Alentejo pork meat with clams, or the purslane soup are some of the most prized dishes in this region.

Wine tasting with Tapas

Come visit winery and, with the passion of those who venture to explore their senses, taste 4 wines and try a major selection of regional flavours. If you want to bring your family, we have a special program for the little ones.

The visit begins with our history. A film in our auditorium invites you to travel back in time to discover how winery was born, its first steps and how the wines are produced.

The people who visit our winery, have the opportunity to go through the different stages of wine making and, in the casks room, they get to know about all of our wines’ production secrets.

The visit ends with a tasting of four 4 wines and a Major selection of regional products, to get to know more about the aromas and flavours of Alentejo.

You can also enjoy the view from the terrace of winery, where great wines are drawn.

Hands-on harvest

In this activity, we invite you to participate in a unique moment of wine production, the grape harvest.

We will go to the vineyards and start by checking the maturity of the different grape varieties. If the sugar concentration is ideal, we will harvest by hand, but only enough not to get tired! After breakfast in the vineyard, the rest of the winemaking work follows.

Back to the winery, you will participate in whatever tasks are taking place- it could be the 'inoculation' of a vat, to start the fermentation, a 'pump over' where the must is pumped over the skins and seeds to aid maceration or a “devatting”, drawing off the wine. As tradition dictates, we then crush the grapes with our bare feet and, of course, taste wine!

At the end, as a souvenir of the experience, a bottle of wine will be offered to each participant.


Highlights:

  • Visit to the vineyards and harvest;
  • Activities in the vineyards, picking the grapes;
  • Snack in the vineyard;
  • Visit to the facilities and winery, with an accompaniment of the wine-making process;
  • Regional lunch, with wine tasting.

Be a winemaker for a day

Join us for a unique wine experience where you create your own wine and become a winemaker for a day. Learn more about different grape varieties and make your own blend during this wine workshop. We will start with a visit to the winery, in order to show the history and our winemaking style.

We will make a tasting of several wines, of which, some single variety, to give some hints of tasting notes.

So, after that, you will be ready to make your own wine. Under the tutelage of an oenologist at the Winery, you will have a chance to blend your own wine and take away a special bottle of your creation with your name on it.

You will have at your disposal pipettes, beakers, funnels, paper & pens, you will start by dividing the tasks among the group. Nothing will be left behind. From the wine blend to the label design, you will have to do everything and our winemaker team will taste it and evaluate your creation.

Guaranteed fun ... a very positive way to spend a moment among friends or family, and why not offer the bottle created to a loved one?

The activity will be accompanied by a plate of snacks.

Discover our wine heritage in a relaxed way and in true Maçanita style!


Highlights:

  • Arrival at the winery and welcome by the producer;
  • Guided tour to the winery and cellars;
  • Workshop “How to create your own wine”;
  • Tasting of products of the region:
    • Alentejo regional bread;
    • Special regional cheese;
    • Olive oil tasting Oliveira Ramos Premium;
  • Take home your own wine in a 375 ml bottle.

Full visit

Join us on a 4×4 tour along the dirt roads of the estate. Absorb the knowledge of our guides and feel proud to call the Alentejo your home.

Discover our special connection to the generous lands of the Alentejo, from which we produce cork, wine, Iberian pork and honey. Immerse yourself deep in the cork oak forest to understand how cork grows and is appreciated in the Alentejo. Feel its ecosystem, drink pure water, smell the flowers and touch the trees. Get out of the jeep to feel the textures, appreciate the view, and learn through experience. At the end of the tour, climb the hillside and enjoy an incredible 360º view, of the boundaries of the estate and the beautiful landscape.

After the jeep tour, discover the world of wines on a guided tour of the estate, where you will visit the winery and tasting room. Then you will enjoy a tasting of wines, accompanied by Iberian pork ham and cheese.

At the end of the morning, filled with activities, you will have lunch in a typical Alentejo restaurant, with a traditional menu of the region, accompanied by Alentejo wines.

Adventure yourself on an off-road journey through the Alentejo's "montado" (cork oak forest)!


Highlights:
